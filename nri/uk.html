What to Expect: Jaipur for British Visitors

Practical tips for UK patients visiting Jaipur

Accommodation

Hotels near hospital: £15-90/night. Budget guesthouses to 4-star hotels. All with WiFi, hot water, English TV. We recommend safe, comfortable options suitable for British guests.

Food & Drink

Indian cuisine (vegetarian-friendly). Continental food at hotels. Coffee shops (Starbucks, Cafe Coffee Day). Always drink bottled water. Avoid street food initially. Hotel restaurants safe choice.

Currency & ATMs

1 GBP = ~105 INR (approx). ATMs accept UK cards. Inform your bank before travel. Keep some cash for small purchases. Most places accept cards. Very affordable for British standards.

Getting Around

We arrange airport pickup. Local apps like Uber/Ola work well. Private car with driver: £25-40/day. Hospital is 2-5 km from recommended hotels. Rickshaws available but negotiate fare first.

Language & Communication

English widely spoken. Our staff fluent in English. Tourist areas very English-friendly. UK mobile roaming expensive - buy local SIM (£8 for month). WhatsApp works on WiFi everywhere.

Sightseeing

Jaipur is UNESCO World Heritage site. Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal must-visits. Shop for textiles, jewelry, handicrafts. Day trips to Agra (Taj Mahal) possible. Safe, tourist-friendly city.

Common Questions from UK Patients

Is the quality comparable to UK private hospitals?

Yes. Our doctors have MS/MDS qualifications (equivalent to UK specialist training). Equipment and materials are same international brands used in UK (Zeiss, Alcon, Straumann, Nobel). Lower costs due to India's economy, not lower quality standards.

Can I claim on my UK private insurance?

Some UK insurers (Bupa, AXA, Vitality) may reimburse international treatment. Check your policy. We provide UK-standard invoices and medical reports. Even without insurance coverage, you save money vs UK private costs.

What about NHS follow-up care when I return?

We provide comprehensive medical records in English format suitable for NHS. Your UK GP can access reports. We also offer remote follow-ups via WhatsApp/video. Most procedures need minimal follow-up. For serious issues, we coordinate with UK doctors.

Is India safe for British tourists?

Jaipur is very safe for tourists. It's a major tourist destination with good infrastructure. Millions of British tourists visit India yearly. We arrange safe hotels, provide guidance, and our staff supports you throughout. Standard travel precautions apply.

How do I manage medications after returning to UK?

We provide enough medication for your recovery period. Prescriptions are in English with generic names your UK pharmacist will recognize. For longer-term medications, your UK GP can continue prescriptions based on our reports.

What if I get ill during my stay?

Jaipur has excellent hospitals and emergency care. We provide medical support 24/7. Travel insurance (which you should get anyway) covers medical emergencies. India's healthcare system is good - many British doctors trained here!
