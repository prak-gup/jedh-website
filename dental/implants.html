What Are Dental Implants?

Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed into the jawbone to replace missing tooth roots. They provide a strong foundation for fixed or removable replacement teeth that match your natural teeth.

Benefits of Dental Implants

  • Look, feel, and function like natural teeth
  • Prevent bone loss and maintain facial structure
  • No damage to adjacent healthy teeth
  • Eat your favorite foods without restriction
  • Improved speech and confidence
  • Long-lasting solution (20+ years with care)

The 3-Stage Implant Process

A systematic approach ensuring optimal results

1

Stage 1: Consultation & Implant Placement

Comprehensive examination with 3D imaging, bone assessment, and treatment planning. The titanium implant is surgically placed into the jawbone under local anesthesia. This procedure typically takes 30-60 minutes per implant.

Duration: 1 day procedure

2

Stage 2: Healing & Osseointegration

The implant fuses with the jawbone through a natural process called osseointegration. During this period, a temporary restoration may be provided. Regular check-ups ensure proper healing.

Duration: 2-4 months healing time

3

Stage 3: Abutment & Crown Placement

Once healing is complete, an abutment (connector) is attached to the implant. Custom-made crown is then placed on the abutment, matching your natural teeth in color, shape, and function. Final adjustments ensure perfect fit and bite.

Duration: 2-3 weeks for crown fabrication

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about dental implants

Are dental implants painful?

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during surgery. Post-operative discomfort is minimal and managed with mild pain medications. Most patients resume normal activities within 2-3 days.

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care and oral hygiene, dental implants can last 20+ years or even a lifetime. The crown may need replacement after 10-15 years due to normal wear. Premium German systems come with extended warranties.

Am I a good candidate for implants?

Most adults with good general and oral health are candidates. You need adequate jawbone density and healthy gums. We'll conduct comprehensive evaluation including 3D scans. If bone is insufficient, we can perform bone grafting.

What's the difference between Korean and German implants?

Both are quality systems. German implants offer advanced surface technology, faster osseointegration, longer warranties, and extensive global research backing. Korean implants are more cost-effective while maintaining good quality. We'll recommend based on your specific needs.

Do you accept insurance for implants?

Yes, we're empaneled with major insurance providers. Coverage varies by policy - some cover up to 50-80% of implant costs. We'll help verify your coverage and process claims. EMI options also available.

Can I get implants in one day?

Immediate loading (teeth in a day) is possible in select cases with good bone quality. However, the traditional 3-stage approach ensures highest success rates. We'll assess your case and recommend the best protocol for optimal long-term results.
