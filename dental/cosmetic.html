Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about cosmetic dentistry

How long does teeth whitening last?

Professional teeth whitening typically lasts 1-3 years depending on your diet and oral hygiene habits. Avoiding staining foods (coffee, tea, red wine) and maintaining good oral care extends results. Touch-up treatments can be done annually to maintain brightness.

Are veneers permanent?

Yes, veneers are considered a permanent treatment because a small amount of enamel must be removed to place them. However, the veneers themselves last 10-15 years and may need replacement due to normal wear. With proper care, many patients enjoy their veneers for even longer.

Is cosmetic dentistry painful?

Most cosmetic dental procedures are minimally invasive and cause little to no discomfort. Local anesthesia is used when needed for procedures like veneer placement. Teeth whitening and bonding typically require no anesthesia. Advanced techniques and technology ensure patient comfort throughout treatment.

What is a Hollywood smile?

A 'Hollywood smile' refers to a perfect, bright, symmetrical smile similar to those seen in celebrities. It typically involves a combination of treatments including professional whitening, veneers on visible teeth, and sometimes orthodontics. The result is a dazzling, camera-ready smile that's natural-looking yet stunning.

How much does a complete smile makeover cost?

The cost of a smile makeover varies greatly depending on the treatments needed and number of teeth involved. Simple cases with whitening and bonding may start around ₹50,000, while comprehensive makeovers with multiple veneers can range from ₹1,50,000 to ₹4,00,000+. We provide detailed cost estimates during consultation and offer EMI options.

Can I see my new smile before treatment?

Absolutely! We use digital smile design technology to create a realistic preview of your new smile. This allows you to see the expected results and make any desired changes before we begin treatment. You can even share the preview with family and friends to get their input. This ensures you're completely confident in your treatment plan.
